## 2. CLIENT HEALTH SCORING SYSTEM

Every active client receives a Health Score updated **weekly** (during sprints) or **monthly** (during retainer). The score runs from 0–100 across four equal dimensions. Any client scoring below **60** triggers immediate escalation to Hasan.

### Scoring Overview

| Dimension | Max Points | Weight |
|---|---|---|
| Engagement Quality | 25 | 25% |
| Results Progress | 25 | 25% |
| Relationship Health | 25 | 25% |
| Value Perception | 25 | 25% |
| **TOTAL** | **100** | **100%** |

---

### Dimension 1: Engagement Quality (0–25 points)

Measures how actively the client is participating in the work — responding to messages, implementing recommendations, and showing up to scheduled calls.

| Score Range | Criteria |
|---|---|
| **22–25 (Excellent)** | Responds to all messages within 24 hours. Has implemented 80%+ of recommendations delivered. Attends every scheduled call on time and comes prepared with questions/updates. Proactively shares business updates without prompting. |
| **16–21 (Good)** | Responds within 48 hours consistently. Has implemented 50–79% of recommendations. Attends most calls (1 missed per month maximum). Occasionally shares unsolicited updates. |
| **10–15 (Moderate)** | Responses take 48–72 hours. Has implemented 25–49% of recommendations. Misses 1–2 calls per month. Rarely engages between touchpoints. |
| **5–9 (Low)** | Responses take 3–7 days or require follow-up. Fewer than 25% of recommendations actioned. Misses most calls or frequently reschedules last-minute. Shows minimal initiative between scheduled touchpoints. |
| **0–4 (Critical)** | Largely unresponsive (7+ days). Has implemented near zero recommendations. Has missed 3+ consecutive scheduled touchpoints. |

**Detection Method:** Track via Notion activity log and call attendance register. Flag when score drops by 5+ points week-over-week.

---

### Dimension 2: Results Progress (0–25 points)

Measures whether agreed KPIs are moving in the right direction and whether the core business constraints identified in the audit are being resolved.

| Score Range | Criteria |
|---|---|
| **22–25 (Excellent)** | 2+ target KPIs have improved measurably since engagement began. At least one core constraint from the audit has been fully resolved. Client can cite specific numbers showing progress. Results are ahead of projected timeline. |
| **16–21 (Good)** | At least 1 KPI showing measurable improvement. Progress is on track with projected timeline. Core constraints are actively being worked and showing early movement. |
| **10–15 (Moderate)** | KPIs are flat but not declining. Constraints are acknowledged but implementation is slow. Some early positive signals visible but not yet quantifiable. |
| **5–9 (Low)** | No measurable KPI movement after 30+ days of engagement. Constraints remain unresolved despite recommendations. Client has not yet taken substantive action on the plan. |
| **0–4 (Critical)** | KPIs are declining. Client has taken no action on any recommendations. No progress visible against any target from the engagement plan. |

**Detection Method:** Pull KPI snapshot monthly from Agent 10 (Performance Data). Compare against the baseline captured during audit. Flag if 2+ KPIs show no movement after 6 weeks.

---

### Dimension 3: Relationship Health (0–25 points)

Measures the quality of trust and the emotional tone of the client relationship.

| Score Range | Criteria |
|---|---|
| **22–25 (Excellent)** | Client openly shares challenges and asks for advice beyond the defined scope. Uses warm, collaborative language in messages. Has referred at least one contact or expressed willingness to do so. Expresses confidence in Numu publicly or privately. |
| **16–21 (Good)** | Communication is positive and professional. Client is candid about what's working and what isn't. No significant complaints or tensions. Relationship feels like a genuine partnership. |
| **10–15 (Moderate)** | Communication is transactional but not hostile. Client tends to stay within narrow scope. Occasional hints of frustration but nothing overt. Limited personal rapport built. |
| **5–9 (Low)** | Communication is tense or guarded. Client has raised complaints (even informally). Responses are clipped. There may be an unresolved expectation mismatch or a missed deliverable in the history. |
| **0–4 (Critical)** | Client has expressed dissatisfaction directly. Communication has become formal or adversarial. Trust has been damaged. Risk of early termination is high. |

**Detection Method:** Review tone of all message threads weekly. Flag any message containing language like "disappointed," "expected," "not getting," "thought you would," or "reconsidering."

---

### Dimension 4: Value Perception (0–25 points)

Measures whether the client views their investment in Numu Consulting as high-ROI rather than a cost to be minimised.

| Score Range | Criteria |
|---|---|
| **22–25 (Excellent)** | Client has spontaneously mentioned ROI or value in conversation. Has requested additional services or asked about upsells. Has told colleagues about working with Numu. Would confidently recommend without being asked. |
| **16–21 (Good)** | Client acknowledges value when asked. Renews or continues engagement without significant hesitation. Sees the retainer as a business investment. Unlikely to shop alternatives. |
| **10–15 (Moderate)** | Client has mixed feelings about value. May have questioned pricing at least once. Hasn't seen enough tangible wins yet to feel fully confident. Sits on the fence about renewal. |
| **5–9 (Low)** | Client has directly or indirectly questioned whether the engagement is worth the cost. May be comparing Numu to cheaper alternatives. Renewal is uncertain. |
| **0–4 (Critical)** | Client has stated they are not seeing value. Has requested a pause, discount, or exit. Considers the retainer a cost, not an investment. Cancellation is imminent without intervention. |

**Detection Method:** Monitor for pricing questions, scope reduction requests, delayed invoice payments, or questions about "what exactly they're paying for." Escalate immediately when 0–9 range is reached on this dimension.

---

## 3. ONBOARDING SEQUENCE — AUDIT CLIENTS

The onboarding window (first 48 hours post-payment) sets the emotional tone for the entire engagement. Speed, professionalism, and genuine warmth are the three non-negotiables.

### Step 1: Welcome Message (Within 2 Hours of Payment Confirmation)

**Trigger:** Payment confirmed by billing system or Stripe webhook.
**Channel:** Email (primary) + WhatsApp/iMessage if mobile number captured.
**Template:** See [Template 01 — Audit Welcome Message](#template-01--audit-welcome-message)

**Checklist:**
- [ ] Address client by first name
- [ ] Reference the specific package purchased
- [ ] Set expectation for audit timeline
- [ ] Introduce the Pre-Audit Questionnaire with a clear deadline
- [ ] Include Hasan's personal sign-off (or reference to the team)
- [ ] Warm, confident tone — not robotic

---

### Step 2: Pre-Audit Questionnaire Delivery (Within 2 Hours of Welcome Message)

**Purpose:** Gather the raw business intelligence needed to make the audit maximally precise and useful. The questionnaire also begins the value demonstration — clients start seeing the depth of Numu's thinking before the audit even begins.

**Delivery Method:** Notion form link or Typeform embedded in a follow-up message.
**Deadline:** Client should return within 48 hours of receiving.
**Template:** See [Template 02 — Pre-Audit Questionnaire Delivery](#template-02--pre-audit-questionnaire-delivery)

**Core Questionnaire Areas:**

1. **Business Overview** — Industry, revenue range, team size, years operating
2. **Primary Revenue Streams** — What they sell, how they sell it, main channels
3. **Current Constraints** — What is the #1 thing preventing faster growth right now?
4. **Recent Attempts** — What have they tried to solve this? What happened?
5. **KPI Baselines** — Conversion rates, monthly revenue, lead volume, CAC, LTV (whatever is relevant)
6. **Team & Resources** — Who handles what, where they are bottlenecked
7. **Goals for This Engagement** — What does success look like in 90 days?
8. **Tools & Systems** — CRM, email platform, analytics, project management
9. **Biggest Fear** — What is the one outcome they're most worried about?
10. **Decision Context** — What made them choose Numu, and what do they most want to see validated?

---

### Step 3: Notion Workspace Setup

**Timeline:** Complete within 4 hours of receiving the questionnaire.
**Purpose:** The client's Notion workspace is their single source of truth for the entire engagement. A well-structured workspace signals professionalism and makes it easy for the client to see exactly what is happening at every stage.

**Notion Workspace Setup Checklist:**

- [ ] Create client workspace using Numu standard template
- [ ] Name format: `[Client Name] — Numu Consulting Workspace`
- [ ] Populate **Client Profile** page with information from questionnaire
- [ ] Create **Audit Tracker** page with status indicators
- [ ] Create **Recommendations Log** — blank, ready for audit findings
- [ ] Create **KPI Dashboard** page — pre-populate baseline metrics from questionnaire
- [ ] Create **Action Items** database with Owner, Due Date, Status, and Priority columns
- [ ] Create **Meeting Notes** database — linked to upcoming calls
- [ ] Create **Resources & Deliverables** folder — add onboarding documents
- [ ] Add client as a Notion guest with comment/view access
- [ ] Send client workspace access link with a brief orientation (see Template 03)
- [ ] Verify client has successfully accessed workspace before audit begins

---

### Step 4: Expectations Document

**Purpose:** Eliminate ambiguity. Define what the client will receive, when, and in what format. This document is referenced whenever scope questions arise and protects both parties.
**Template:** See [Template 04 — Expectations Document](#template-04--expectations-document)

**Core Sections of the Expectations Document:**

1. **What You'll Receive** — Deliverables list with format and timeline
2. **What We Need From You** — Response times, information access, decision-making

## 4. DURING AUDIT DELIVERY

### Progress Update Cadence

| Day | Action |
|---|---|
| Day 1 | Send "Audit in Progress" confirmation message (Template 05) |
| Day 3 | Send mid-audit progress signal: key area being reviewed, 1–2 interesting observations (no findings yet) |
| Day 5–7 | Complete audit. Record Loom walkthrough of findings. |
| Day 7 | Deliver audit report to client Notion workspace + email notification |

### Loom Walkthrough Protocol

The Loom walkthrough transforms the audit report from a document into a conversation. It makes findings feel collaborative, not clinical.

**Loom Recording Checklist:**
- [ ] Maximum 20 minutes — tight and purposeful
- [ ] Open by naming 1 major finding that surprised you (builds credibility)
- [ ] Walk through each constraint area with screen share of the report
- [ ] Use client's business name and specific numbers throughout
- [ ] End with 2–3 clear next steps — build natural bridge toward Sprint conversation
- [ ] Upload to Notion workspace and embed in Audit Report page
- [ ] Send notification to client with direct Loom link (Template 06)

### Live Strategy Session Scheduling

Within 2 hours of audit delivery, send the session scheduling link (Template 07). This session is where Sprint conversion happens — it must be positioned as a working session, not a sales call.

**Session Agenda Template:**
- (0:00–5:00) Review client's initial reaction to the audit findings
- (5:00–20:00) Deep dive on top 2 constraints — discuss root causes
- (20:00–35:00) Walk through Sprint plan — what would be tackled, in what order, and why
- (35:00–45:00) Address questions. Discuss investment and timeline.
- (45:00–50:00) Decision or clear next step agreed

### Feedback Collection

After audit delivery, send a short 3-question feedback request (Template 08). This serves three purposes: it shows confidence in the work, it surfaces any concerns early, and it generates data to improve future audits.

## 6. SPRINT MANAGEMENT PROTOCOL

### Sprint Structure Overview

A Numu Sprint is a focused, time-bound engagement (typically 30 days) designed to implement the highest-priority recommendations from the audit and drive measurable results.

**Sprint Kick-Off Checklist:**
- [ ] Update Notion workspace with Sprint scope, objectives, and KPI targets
- [ ] Schedule all 4 weekly calls in advance and send calendar invites
- [ ] Send Sprint Kick-Off message (Template 13)
- [ ] Define success criteria in writing — agreed by both parties
- [ ] Confirm client's implementation bandwidth (who on their team will execute)

### 4 Weekly Call Structure

**Call 1 — Sprint Launch (Week 1)**
- Review Sprint objectives and success criteria
- Confirm priorities and assign ownership of each action item
- Identify any blockers or risks upfront
- Set expectations for Week 1 deliverables

**Call 2 — Mid-Sprint Check-In (Week 2)**
- Review Week 1 implementation progress
- Address any blockers with specific solutions
- Recalibrate priorities if needed based on early data
- Preview Week 3 focus areas

**Call 3 — Momentum Review (Week 3)**
- Review early KPI movement — is progress on track?
- Double down on what's working
- Make tactical adjustments where needed
- Begin planting seeds for post-Sprint continuation

**Call 4 — Sprint Debrief & Results Review (Week 4)**
- Present a full Sprint Results Summary (built with Agent 10 data)
- Highlight all measurable wins against baselines
- Introduce the next phase options — Retainer or second Sprint
- Make the transition offer natural, not pressured

### Implementation Support Between Calls

- Respond to all client messages within 4 business hours
- Proactively share relevant resources, templates, or examples when client encounters a new challenge
- Add all decisions and action items to Notion after every interaction
- If a client is stuck, offer a 15-minute async Loom solution rather than waiting for the next scheduled call

### Post-Sprint Debrief Report

Deliver a written Post-Sprint Debrief to the client Notion workspace within 48 hours of Call 4.

**Debrief Report Contents:**
1. **Sprint Objectives** — what we set out to accomplish
2. **Work Completed** — complete list of deliverables and implementations
3. **Results Achieved** — KPI movement with before/after comparison
4. **Constraints Resolved** — from the original audit list
5. **Remaining Opportunities** — what comes next and why it matters
6. **Recommended Next Step** — specific Retainer or Sprint proposal

## 7. RETAINER MANAGEMENT

### Monthly Report Structure

Every retainer client receives a Monthly Impact Report delivered by the 5th of each month.

**Report Sections:**
1. **Executive Summary** — 3 bullet points: biggest win, key challenge, top priority for next month
2. **KPI Dashboard** — Current vs. baseline vs. last month for all tracked metrics
3. **Work Completed** — Full list of deliverables and implementations in the month
4. **Recommendations Implemented** — What was actioned and what results followed
5. **Upcoming Focus** — Plan for next 30 days
6. **Open Items** — Anything awaiting client decision or input

**Delivery:** Upload to Notion workspace. Send notification email (Template 14). Walk through on monthly review call.

### Quarterly Review Protocol

Every 90 days, conduct a formal Quarterly Business Review (QBR).

**QBR Agenda:**
- (0:00–10:00) Celebrate wins — quantify the impact of the past quarter
- (10:00–25:00) Review remaining constraints from original audit
- (25:00–35:00) Market/business context — has anything material changed?
- (35:00–45:00) Present next quarter's strategic priorities
- (45:00–55:00) Discuss retainer scope — is the current structure still optimal?
- (55:00–60:00) Referral conversation — natural, not pressured

**QBR Deliverable:** A written QBR Summary document in Notion within 48 hours of the session.

### Proactive Value Delivery

Between scheduled calls, Agent 09 delivers unsolicited value at least **twice per month**. Examples:

- Share a relevant article or data point with a 2-sentence personalised commentary on why it matters for their specific business
- Send a Loom video pointing out a quick-win opportunity spotted during routine work
- Alert client to a competitor movement or market shift they should know about
- Share a template or tool that directly addresses a challenge they mentioned on the last call

This "proactive value" approach is what separates a Numu retainer from a transactional agency relationship.

## 8. REFERRAL SYSTEM

### Philosophy: Colleague Introduction, Not Cold Request

Numu does not ask clients for referrals — it creates the conditions in which clients want to introduce colleagues. The distinction matters. A request creates obligation; an invitation creates partnership.

**Target:** 3–5 qualified referrals per quarter from the active client base.

### Timing: Peak Satisfaction Windows

The referral conversation is most effective at three specific moments:

1. **After a quick win** — client has just seen a tangible result (first KPI improvement, constraint resolved)
2. **Post-Sprint Debrief** — end of a sprint with measurable outcomes in hand
3. **Quarterly Review** — looking back at 90 days of documented progress

**Never initiate a referral conversation when:**
- Client health score is below 70
- A concern or complaint is unresolved
- The client has just asked a value-related question

### Referral Conversation Template

**Script (verbal, on call):**

> "One thing I want to mention — a lot of our best client relationships have come from introductions by people like yourself, who've experienced what it's like to work with us. If there's anyone in your network — another founder, a colleague, a peer in a mastermind — who you think could benefit from the kind of clarity the audit gave you, we'd love to have a conversation with them. No pressure at all, just thought it was worth mentioning."

**Follow-up message if client expresses interest:** See [Template 15 — Referral Introduction Request](#template-15--referral-introduction-request)

### Referral Incentives

- **No financial commission** (maintains professional integrity and prevents misaligned introductions)
- **Recognition:** Featured as a "Numu Insider" — referenced (with permission) as a trusted source of the introduction
- **Value-add:** Complimentary additional strategy session for any retainer referral that converts

## 9. RED FLAG RISK INDICATORS

The following seven indicators signal elevated churn risk. Each has a specific detection trigger and intervention protocol.

---

### Red Flag 1: Response Time Degradation

**What it looks like:** Client was previously responding within 24 hours. Over the past 2 weeks, responses are taking 48–72 hours or requiring follow-up messages.

**Detection:** Monitored weekly via message thread review. Flagged if average response time doubles from their personal baseline.

**Intervention Protocol:**
- Do not send another standard touchpoint message
- Send a brief, human check-in (Template — RF1 Check-In)
- If no response within 72 hours, escalate to Hasan for a direct personal outreach

---

### Red Flag 2: Missed Consecutive Calls

**What it looks like:** Client misses 2 or more scheduled calls in a row, even with rescheduling.

**Detection:** Call attendance log in Notion. Auto-flag after 2nd consecutive missed/rescheduled call.

**Intervention Protocol:**
- Do not send another calendar link
- Send a direct, empathetic message acknowledging the pattern (Template — RF2 Call Gap)
- Offer a format change: async Loom + written response instead of live call
- Review client's current implementation load — may need to reduce scope temporarily

---

### Red Flag 3: Implementation Stall

**What it looks like:** 3+ recommendations from the audit or Sprint plan have been unactioned for 21+ days despite being agreed priorities.

**Detection:** Weekly review of Notion Action Items database. Flag when 3+ items remain "Pending" for 21+ days.

**Intervention Protocol:**
- Acknowledge the stall without blame — ask what barriers exist
- Offer a "priority simplification" — reduce to one single next action the client can complete this week
- If the stall continues, assess whether the recommendations are realistic for the client's bandwidth and adjust the plan

---

### Red Flag 4: Value Question Raised

**What it looks like:** Client asks "What exactly are we getting for this?" or "I'm not sure this is working" or "Can you remind me what we've done this month?"

**Detection:** Keyword monitoring in all message channels. Immediate flag on any variation of value or cost challenge language.

**Intervention Protocol:**
- Respond within 2 hours — do not let this sit
- Pull a quick impact summary from Agent 10 (all work done, all KPI movement)
- Respond with specific evidence, not reassurance (Template — RF4 Value Reaffirmation)
- Schedule a call within 5 business days for a live conversation
- Update health score and alert Hasan if score drops below 60

---

### Red Flag 5: Billing Friction

**What it looks like:** Invoice is 7+ days overdue without communication. Client requests a payment pause or asks about cancellation terms.

**Detection:** Billing system flag at 7 days overdue. Immediate flag on any cancellation or pause inquiry.

**Intervention Protocol:**
- Do not chase payment in an aggressive tone — lead with relationship
- Send a gentle, personal message within 48 hours of overdue flag
- If client raises pause/cancellation, escalate to Hasan immediately
- Document all billing conversations in Notion

---

### Red Flag 6: Scope Creep Resentment

**What it looks like:** Client begins requesting work outside the agreed scope and shows frustration when it cannot be delivered immediately. Or client begins expressing that the engagement "isn't covering enough."

**Detection:** Review of message threads for requests outside documented scope. Flag if client makes 3+ out-of-scope requests in one month.

**Intervention Protocol:**
- Acknowledge the desire for broader support (it signals engagement)
- Clarify current scope with reference to the Expectations Document — warm, not defensive
- Frame the out-of-scope work as an upgrade opportunity — route to Agent 03 for appropriate upsell package
- If frustration persists, escalate to Hasan for a direct scope alignment conversation

---

### Red Flag 7: Social Disengagement

**What it looks like:** Client stops engaging with Numu content (previously engaged), no longer responds warmly to messages, or gives one-word answers where they previously wrote detailed responses.

**Detection:** Monitor tone and length of client responses week-over-week. Flag if 3+ consecutive interactions are noticeably shorter or cooler than the client's baseline communication style.

**Intervention Protocol:**
- Do not send a standard template message
- Hasan writes a direct, personal 3-sentence message asking how things are going beyond the engagement
- Goal is to reconnect as a person before addressing the professional dynamic
- If warmth does not return within 2 weeks, treat as at-risk and escalate

### Primary Escalation Threshold: Health Score Below 60

**Trigger:** Any client's total health score drops below 60 out of 100.

**Escalation Package — what Agent 09 prepares for Hasan:**

2. **Current Health Score** — Overall score + breakdown by all 4 dimensions
3. **Score Trend** — Last 4 weekly scores to show trajectory
4. **Root Cause Assessment** — Which dimension is weakest, and why (specific evidence)
5. **Recent Interaction Log** — Last 5 client messages and Agent 09 responses
6. **Red Flags Active** — Which of the 7 red flags are currently triggered
7. **Recommended Intervention** — Agent 09's suggested approach for Hasan to consider
8. **Draft Message** — A personal message from Hasan to the client, ready to send with minimal editing

**Escalation Format:** Notion page titled `ESCALATION — [Client Name] — [Date]` created in the internal Numu workspace. Hasan notified by direct message immediately.

### Secondary Escalations (Direct to Hasan, regardless of health score)

- Any client mentions cancellation or requests refund
- Any billing dispute or payment refusal
- Any complaint that names Hasan specifically
- Any client expresses a threat (reputational, legal, or otherwise)
- Any client requests a scope reduction that would reduce revenue by 50%+

### Escalation Guiding Principle

Hasan is the relationship backstop for Numu Consulting. When a client is at risk, the fastest path to recovery is almost always a direct, personal conversation with Hasan. Agent 09's role in escalation is to prepare that conversation — not to resolve it independently.
