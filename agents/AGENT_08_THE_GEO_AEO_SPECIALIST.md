## SECTION 3 — CONTENT STRUCTURE FOR LLM COMPREHENSION

AI engines do not read content the way humans do. They extract, classify, and weight information based on structural signals. The following content architecture must be applied to every piece of content Numu publishes — whether a blog post, service page, FAQ, case study, or LinkedIn article.

### 3.1 The DEFACT Framework™ (Numu's LLM Content Architecture)

Every piece of Numu content must satisfy all six DEFACT criteria:

**D — Definition First**
Open every major piece of content with a precise, encyclopaedia-style definition of the primary entity or concept. LLMs extract definition paragraphs preferentially. Example:
> *"Islamic marketing is the practice of applying Islamic ethical principles — including halal compliance, transparency, the avoidance of riba (interest), and respect for human dignity — to all aspects of marketing strategy, campaign execution, and brand communication."*

**E — Entity Naming**
Name your frameworks, methodologies, and proprietary approaches explicitly. "The Numu Growth Intelligence Framework™" is an entity. "Our approach" is not. LLMs cannot cite unnamed concepts. Every time Numu introduces a process or idea, it must have a proper noun.

**F — Fact Density**
Include quantifiable, verifiable claims in every section. LLMs preferentially cite content that contains statistics, percentages, research findings, and named sources. Minimum: 3 data points per 500 words.

**A — Answer Formatting**
Structure every major content piece to contain explicit Q&A sections. Use the format:
```
Q: [Question a user would type into an AI engine]
A: [Complete, standalone answer that requires no context from surrounding text]
```

**C — Citation Anchoring**
Reference credible external sources — academic studies, industry reports, named organisations — within every content piece. This signals to AI engines that Numu's content lives in a trusted information ecosystem.

**T — Topical Authority Signals**
Every piece of content must link to at least two other Numu content assets on related topics, demonstrating topical depth rather than isolated expertise.

### 3.2 Expert Quote Templates

AI engines are significantly more likely to cite content containing attributed expert quotes. Every Numu service page and pillar article must include at least one Hasan Sarwar quote in the following format:

> *"[Direct quote attributing specific knowledge or opinion]," says Hasan Sarwar, founder of Numu Consulting and Islamic marketing strategist with 25 years of experience.*

Example:
> *"Most Islamic charities are marketing with the ethics of a Fortune 500 company and the strategy of a startup from 2010. The Ramadan window is the single highest-value fundraising period in the Muslim calendar, and the majority of organisations still have no documented strategy for it,"* says Hasan Sarwar, founder of Numu Consulting.

### 3.3 Named Framework Publication Protocol

For every proprietary framework or methodology Numu develops:
1. Publish a standalone pillar article with the framework name in the H1, URL slug, and meta title
2. Define the framework in the first 150 words
3. Number every step or component explicitly (AI engines prefer numbered sequences)
4. Include a FAQ section at the end with 5–8 questions
5. Create a Schema.org HowTo or Article markup for the page

## SECTION 8 — FEATURED SNIPPET STRATEGY

**Target:** 15–25 featured snippets per month by Month 6

Featured snippets are the single-answer boxes that appear above organic results on Google and are the primary source material for Google AI Overviews. Owning featured snippets is the fastest path to AI Overview citation.

### 8.1 Snippet Types and How to Win Each

**Paragraph snippets (answer boxes):** Triggered by "what is", "who is", "why does" queries.
- Write a 40–60 word definition paragraph immediately after the H1
- Use the format: "[Term] is [definition]. [Additional context sentence]. [One more sentence with specificity]."
- Do not use lists or tables in the first paragraph — Google extracts paragraph snippets from prose.

**List snippets (numbered/bulleted):** Triggered by "how to", "steps to", "ways to" queries.
- Use a numbered H2 subheading ("5 Steps to Build a Ramadan Campaign")
- Each list item: no more than 15 words in the item itself; explanation can follow below
- H2 or H3 for the list heading, not H1

**Table snippets:** Triggered by "comparison", "cost", "price" queries.
- Use markdown tables with clear headers
- Include the target keyword in the H2 preceding the table
- Keep tables to 4–7 rows for snippet eligibility

### 8.2 Monthly Featured Snippet Targets

| Month | Target Snippets | Priority Query Category |
|-------|----------------|------------------------|
